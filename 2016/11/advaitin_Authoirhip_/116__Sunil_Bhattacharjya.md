 Dear friends,



 Since Vacaspati Mishra wrote Bhamati, Sri Vidyashankaar (who
 was born much later than Adi Shankaracharya) has to be left
 out, but was there not any other Shankarachaya, who could
 have done it with the same diction. I understand that there
 were five Shankaracharyas,
 who are considered by some, as the reicarnations of Adi
 Shankaracharya, i.e., these few Shankaracharyas were so very
 much similar to Adi Shankaracharya in their intellectual
 stature.



 May be the treatment of the Panchikarana and other topics
 may give some help. As Sri Sureshvaracharya also wrote on
 Panchikarana, the bhasyakara could have been Adi
 Shankaracharya. The mention of Purnavarman (alias Haala),
 the Magadha king of the Satavahana
 dynasty, in the BS Bhasya, may also support this. 



 Regards,

 Sunil KB

 Dear friends,







 Many of us, if not all of us, know that the Brahmasutra

 (BS) was composed by Badarayana Vyasa and not by

 Krishnadvaipayana Vyasa or Vedavyasa. Has any work been
 done

 on the authorship of the first BS-bahhya, though it is

 generally attributed to Adi Shankara? I mean, has anybody

 looked for internal evidences to establish that it was

 surely by Adi Shankara, or it could be by any other
 Shakara

 such as Abhinavashankara or Vidyashankara or
 Kripashankara,

 whom some consider to be an avatara of Adi Shankara. 







 Regards,



 Sunil KB
