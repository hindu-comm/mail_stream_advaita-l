> Sri Raghav prabhuji has given beautiful explanation / justification that
> since ramaNa was a brAhmin by birth, his realization was through shAstra
> only and those shAstra vAkya-s are there in his daily sadhyAvaNdanaM.
> Really a well thought out explanation.  But question is whether ramaNa
> himself acknowledged this in his teachings??  Whether ramaNa himself agreed
> that his 'first' experience of self at his uncle's house is the result of
> his daily saNdhyA??  I don’t think so.  Nevertheless, it is a good
> explanation by other shrOtreeya brAhmin/s who want to see ramaNa's
> realization in the light of veda and vedAnta adhyayana since he was a
> brAhmin by birth and for the dvija-s vedA-s are the only pramANa, vedAnta
> vAkya shravaNa is the only means.  But what about others other than
> dvija-s, I think ramaNa himself declared that his mother and cow Lakshmi
> attained mukti after death.  I don’t think it is possible for us to bring
> them too in the ambit of vedic tradition to say that they have realized
> through veda-s or vedAnta vAkya janita jnAna.  And what about Nisargadatta
> maharaj, Shrdi Saibaaba, parama haMsa etc.  who are nothing less than
> brahmavidvarishTa-s according to their respective followers.  Can we drag
> them also to traditional boundaries to uphold the shAstras' antya
> praMANatva when it comes to realization??  Again, I don’t think it is
> sensible.  And as Sri sarma prabhuji rightly observed, we have somany
> Atmaikatva jnAnis who are out of the boundaries of orthodox  veda/vedAnta
> tradition.  Just to bring them within the fold of vedic dharma we have to
> invariably guess that they have studied shAstra in their previous janma/s
> since there is no other way other than veda mArga one can realize the
> Atman.  Which obviously sounds dogmatic and conservative view of
> traditionalists for the outsiders.
>
> Just thinking all these as an outsider without holding  any traditional
> flag :-)
