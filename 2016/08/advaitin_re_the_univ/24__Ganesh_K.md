 The
 universe is Śiva's Eight-fold form - Harivamśa


 In the Sridakshinamurti stotram of
 Shankaracharya the ninth verse is:
 भूरम्भांस्यनलोऽनिलोंऽबरमहर्ना
 थो हिमांशुः
 पुमान्इत्याभाति
 चराचरात्मकमिदं
 यस्यैव
 मूर्त्यष्टकम्।नान्यत्किञ्चन
 विद्यते विमृशतां
 यस्मात्परस्माद्विभोःतस्मै
 श्रीगुरुमूर्तय नम इदं
 श्रीदक्षिणामूर्तये॥९॥He, whose eight-fold forms are, the
 ‘earth, water, fire, air, ether, sun, moon andjIva’, and who manifests Himself as
 this universe of the moveable and the immovable objects,
 the Supreme all-pervading Lord, there exists nothing to
 reflect upon … to Him, the divine teacher,
 shrIdakShiNAmUrti is this prostration.
 For a detailed explanation of the
 above see:http://advaita-academy.org/
 shri-dakshinamurti-stotram- part-6/
 It is no surprise that the above
 concept that the universal form, viśvarūpa, is that of
 Lord Śiva, just as it is also stated to be of Lord Viśṇu
 (Bh.gita 11th ch.), by Veda Vyāsa Himself. That Veda
 Vyasa holds Hari and Hara to be no different from each other
 is yet again, for the umpteenth time, evidenced by the
 following verse in the
 Harivamśa3.85.21:

 The
 first word of the above translation aught to be:
 पृथ्वीसहित.
 It is also significant that
 Shankaracharya has cited verses from this very section of
 the Harivamśa in the Vishnusahasra nama bhashya to
 emphasize Hari-Hara / Trimūrti abheda.
 The above verse is preceded
 by:

 This chapter is a prelude to Śiva
 visiting Kriṣṇa who is in penance at Kailāsa to please
 Śiva and obtain boon. The last verse in that chapter
 is:

 It is also significant that Shankara
 in the VSN bhāṣya for the name 'Rudra' (114) has
 cited another verse from the pen of Veda Vyasa, the Shiva
 purana, to say: Shiva is the parama kāraṇam. This is
 reflected in the above cited verse as
 'ādikartā'.
 Since Veda Vyasa holds that Hari and
 Hara are but one principle in two forms (as reiterated in
 the Harivamśa too apart from the Mahabharata), it is no
 contradiction to hold that the viśvarūpa is depicted by
 Veda Vyasa himself as that of Hari and Hara in the Bh.Gita
 and the Harivamśa
 respectively.regardssubrahmanian.v
