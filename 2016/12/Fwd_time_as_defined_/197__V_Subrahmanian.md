> There is a reading that I have heard: For the second half above, it is:
>
> सा सैव प्रक्रियेह स्यात् साध्वी सा चानवस्थिता ।
>
> In the Panchadashi, Vidyaranya paraphrases that verse of Sureshvaracharya
thus:
>
> यया यया भवेत् पुंसां व्युत्पत्तिः प्रत्यगा- त्मनि ।
> सा  सैव प्रक्रियेह स्यात्  साध्वीत्या- चार्यभाषितम् ॥ ७३॥
>
> There is simply no limit to the prakriya-s. Thus, what is found in the
śruti and the bhāṣya is not exhaustive; it is upalakṣaṇa.  A present day
Acharya can always devise a prakriya to suit his pupils of a particular
time and clime.
