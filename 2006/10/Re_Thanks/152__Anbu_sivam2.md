>
> The mahAbhArata has very brief descriptions of devApi. The kuru dynasty
> king
> pratIpa had three sons, devApi, valhika and Santanu. In the Adi parva of
> the
> mahAbhArata, it is merely stated that devApi went to the forest as a
> hermit
> at a young age, while valhika became the king of the western kingdom of
> valhika. In a later parva, dhRtarAshTra gives duryodhana a more detailed
> account. pratIpa wanted to name devApi as the crown prince, but his
> ministers advised him against it, because devApi had some skin disease and
> a
> king was supposed to be blemishless in body. Upon hearing this, devApi
> decided to renounce his claim arising from first birth and went away to
> the
> forest. valhika inherited his maternal grandfather's kingdom of valhika.
> Thus, Santanu inherited pratIpa's kingdom.
