Sir,

It is a sampradaaya SlOkam. There are various sampradaaya-s and various
versions.

It is a great SlOkam set in anushTup, to offer namaskaaram to the
guru-parampara. There are many people who put their guru's name in place of
"asmadaachaarya" if it sets in the chandas. If it doesn't, they will try to
tweak other letters and fit it in :)


2011/3/21 Srikanta Narayanaswami <srikanta.narayanaswami at yahoo.com>

> The slokah actually reads,:

 Sadashiva samarambham Shankaracharya madhyagam(it is not madhyamam)
> asmadacarya paryantam vande aguru paramparam!!
>
> and on vyasa,
>
> Shankaram Shankaracharyam kesavam Badarayanam!
> sutra bhashya krtau vande Bhagavantau punah punah!!
>
> N.Srikanta.
