> why the name. I have to see it in my laptop. Anyway, what is denied with
> respect to the atman is seeing etc. with respect to an external object
> because there is none and also no sense or mind for perception. But the
> names and forms are present in atman as non-different from itself. It is not
> my opinion but sruti defines Brahman that way, which Sankara quotes (e.g.
> BSBh 2.1.14). That this is undifferentiated knowledge (jnapti) is Brahman is
> also stated in smrti jnapti matram (e.g. SBh). Atman is beyond knowing and
> not knowing. It is advaya jnanam.
