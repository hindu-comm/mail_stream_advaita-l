 Ramana Maharshi also equates avidyA and mAyA. Two instances in the "Collected Works":

(1) Book "Self-enquiry":

“Therefore, the ‘I-consciousness’ which at first arises in respect of the body is referred to variously as
self-conceit (tarbodham), egoity (ahankara), nescience (avidya), maya, impurity (mala), and individual soul (jiva).”

(2) Translation of the Vivekachudamani:

“The Supreme (Brahman) has a wonderful shakti (power or energy) known as ‘the undifferentiated’,
‘ignorance’, ‘maya’, etc.”
