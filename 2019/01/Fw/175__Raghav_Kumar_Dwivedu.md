> Namaste Bhaskarji ji
> All prakriyas have to be as per shAstra alone. That's quite true. But the
> way these can be taught are innumerable. Take the dRg-drShya Viveka process
> (although it's only part of the story, still) , which can be taught and
> explained in numerous different ways. Use of modern words and tools like
> awareness, witness, being objective to thoughts and emotions or
> 'present-monent- awareness', even the red-rag-word for many viz.,
> 'experience'  etc., are all allowed with proper context during the teaching
> process as long as we stay within the overall parameters of advaita
> sAmpradAya viz., operation of shabda pramANa through adyAropa-apavAda,
> giving karma yoga its due and so on. The soundness of a teaching method
> depends purely on the authenticity of the teacher and whether he is
> proficient in bhAShya. It does not depend on his use or non-use of specific
> words and concepts which may strike us as more important.
>
> If we straitjacket the words and methods permissible during Advaita
> teaching  overly tightly, then the word 'anavasthitaa' (literally
> 'not-fixed' i.e., variegated or diverse) would not have been there.
>
> Om
> Raghav
