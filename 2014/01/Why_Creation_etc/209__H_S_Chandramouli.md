<<  But for an awakened person, nothing regarding the dream is of interest
- in fact, nothing of the dream is even real enough to warrant his interest
or curiosity. >>

The whole of the Shruti is only for this , " to awaken the person " .

<<  for those trapped in the world some sort of explanation would help,
like drishti-shristi etc.  >>

Not mere explanation, but a way out to get out of the deeprooted conviction
, extending over millions of births , that samsara is TRUE .

> My understanding goes along these lines:

>
> There is no creation, but to explain this apparent appearance/existence of
> the world we resort to drshti-shrsti and other theories. As long as a
> person is trapped in the dream world, he needs explanation regarding the
> dream world, its objects, its ways and rules, its logic and laws, etc. But
> for an awakened person, nothing regarding the dream is of interest - in
> fact, nothing of the dream is even real enough to warrant his interest or
> curiosity.
>
> This is also what Raman Maharishi believed - that even though ajata vada
> is the ultimate truth, for those trapped in the world some sort of
> explanation would help, like drishti-shristi etc.

> This is what I feel. Feel free to disagree.
