> Yes probably but we have to ask whether we are using
> samadhi losely or as
> a technical term.  In Samkhya/Yoga samadhi is the
> final state when the
> turmoil of the mind has ceased.  Chittavrttinirodhah
> as the Yogasutras
> call it.  It can be savikalpa (retaining mental
> concepts) or nirvikalpa
> (where there are no mental concepts.)
>
> For Advaita Vedanta even nirvikalpa samadhi is at
> best the penultimate
> stage.  The freezing of the mental state is not
> enough, there must also be
> the positive knowledge of the oneness of Brahman.
>
> But especially after Samkhya/Yoga became absorbed
> into Vedanta, this
> distinction wasn't always scrupulously maintained.
>

What is that 'input' required to have that '+ve'
