Namaste
It's interesting that Sri Ramanuja also accepts Brahman as devoid of
delimitation by space, time and objects. अनन्तपदं
देशकवलर्स्तुपररच्छेदरहितं(त) स्वरूपम्।
(Although i understand svagatabheda in brahman is accepted by him.)

It looks like the explanation of the above sribhAShya passage has a typo.

"However, the guṇa-s have
vastupariccheda—limitation. This *vastupariccheda* exists only in the case
of Brahman’s essential nature (svarūpa)."

Should it not read *vastuparucchedarAhityam* exists only in Brahman and not
in its guNas?

Om
