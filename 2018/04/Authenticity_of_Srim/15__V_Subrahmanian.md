On Tue, Apr 3, 2018 at 12:54 PM, sreenivasa murthy via Advaita-l <
advaita-l at lists.advaita-vedanta.org> wrote:

> Dear friends,  It is immaterial whether Srimad Bhagavatam is authentic or
> not. What is important to consisder is whether  what has been taught in it
> is authentic or not. That is all. It will be more profittable and fruitful
> if one engages himself in finding out about the authenticity of the
> teachings contained in it.
>

Very well said. This alone should be our criterion in deciding whether a
text is valid for Vedanta or not. That is why texts like
Sridakshinamurtistotram, Vakyavrtti, Mayapanchakam, etc. are acceptable for
Vedantins whether or not it is true that Shankaracharya authored them.
