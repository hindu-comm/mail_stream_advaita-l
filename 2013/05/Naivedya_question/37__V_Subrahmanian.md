Here are some points to be noted:


   1. नादत्ते कस्यचित्पापं न चैव सुकृतं विभुः । अज्ञानेनावृतं ज्ञानं तेन
   मुह्यन्ति जन्तवः ॥ १५ ॥ The bhashyam: न आदत्ते न च गृह्णाति भक्तस्यापि
   कस्यचित् पापम्। न चैव आदत्ते सुकृतं भक्तैः प्रयुक्तं विभुः। किमर्थं तर्हि
   भक्तैः पूजादिलक्षणं यागदानहोमादिकं च सुकृतं प्रयुज्यते इत्याह -- अज्ञानेन
   आवृतं ज्ञानं विवेकविज्ञानम्, तेन मुह्यन्ति 'करोमि कारयामि भोक्ष्ये भोजयामि'
   इत्येवं मोहं गच्छन्ति अविवेकिनः संसारिणो जन्तवः। ।।5.15।।
   2. In the above Bh.Gita verse/bhashyam it is said that the Lord really
   does not accept anyone's sin or good deeds.  Then, why do people engage in
   pUja, yAga, dAna, homa, etc. that are good deeds? It is due to their
   ignorance: 'I am a doer, enjoyer/experiencer of my karma and will also make
   others experience.'
   3. For the eradication of ignorance stated above, one should get samyak
   jnAnam of the form 'I am not a doer/enjoyer at all periods of time' as
   stated in the BSB 'pUrvasiddha kartRtva bhoktRtva viparItam hi.....'
   4. This is called by the special Bh.Gita name: naiShkarmyam.
   5. The Bh.gita itself says: न कर्मणामनारम्भान्नैष्कर्म्यं पुरुषोऽश्नुते
   । न च सन्न्यसनादेव सिद्धिं समधिगच्छति ॥३. ४ ॥
   6. Thus, the means to attain to the state of naiShkarmyam, our true
   nature, is by engaging in niShkAmakarma AcharaNam.  This is called karma
   yoga in the Gita.
   7. This is the path taught by the Vedanta to attain absolute freedom
   from ignorance: naiShkarmya siddhiH.
