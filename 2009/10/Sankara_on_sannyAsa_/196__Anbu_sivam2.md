> I am in complete agreement with the views of Jaldarji.
>
> My point is that the concept of Aashrama is not divorced from the varna but
> is part and parcel of it.  I REITERATE THAT ONLY BRAHMINS ARE QUALIFIED FOR
> THE SANYASA AASHRAMA.
>
> The purpose of Sanyasa Aashrama is to transcend the samsaara.  *Such
> transending of samsaara is available for other varnas too but by such
> non-gnana means as karma, yoga and bhakthi maargas by which they transcend
> samsaara if they had not performed any kaamya karma.*  If they had
> performed
> kaamya karma then it will bear fruit for them to enjoy in heaven and then
> they will be born again.  No one in heaven or pithruloka can attain Moksha
> but only those in Bhuloka therfore those in heaven and in the world of
> manes
> will be born again in bhuloka.
>
> The Brahmins are not qualified for non-gnana path to transcend samsaara.
> If
> a Brahmin follows these maargas then he will be born again.   Kaamya karma
> will bring them heaven and then rebirth into any of the varnas or other
> beings.  Nishkaama karma will bring them a birth as a Brahmin with a Guru
> calling him at opportune moment.
>
> *Adi Sankara's exhortion that only by Gnaana can one lliberate is addressed
> to the Brahmins.*  It would vitiate the varnasrama dharma if he had
> addressed this to any other varna.
