>
> In his commentary on Vichara Sagara, Swami Vasudeva Brahmendra Saraswati
> makes the following observations on the DSV of Sri MS, pp 224, foot notes
>
>
>
> << किञ्चास्मिन्  पक्षे जीवेश्वरादयः  षट्  पदार्थाः  स्वरूपतोऽनादयः
> अङ्गीकृताः ।
>
>
>
>  " जीव  ईशो  विशध्दा  चित् तथाजीवेशयोर्भिदा । अविद्या तच्चितोर्योगः
> षडस्माकमनादयः ॥" इति ॥
>
>
>
> तत्र  ब्रह्मणि पारमार्थिकसत्ताऽस्ति । ब्रह्मभिन्नपञ्चके ( प्रपंचे )
> ब्यावहारिकसत्ता वर्तते । प्रवाहरूपेणानादावितरस्मिन्  सकलकारयप्रपञ्चे
> प्रातिभासिकसत्ताविद्यते । >>
>
>
>
> << ki~nchAsmin  pakShe jIveshvarAdayaH  ShaT  padArthAH
> svarUpato.anAdayaH a~NgIkRRitAH |
>
>
>
> " jIva  Isho  vishadhdA  chit tathAjIveshayorbhidA | avidyA
> tachchitoryogaH  ShaDasmAkamanAdayaH ||" iti ||
>
>
>
> tatra  brahmaNi pAramArthikasattA.asti | brahmabhinnapa~nchake ( prapaMche
> ) byAvahArikasattA vartate | pravAharUpeNAnAdAvitarasmin
> sakalakArayaprapa~nche prAtibhAsikasattAvidyate | >>
>
>
>
> Translation of last sentence (may not be literal)  << Here
> pAramArthikasattA exists in Brahman. In the other five which are different
> from Brahman ( I leave the word prapanchE untranslated), there is
> vyAvahArikasattA. In all the other kAryaprapancha, prAtibhAsikasattA exists.
> >>
>
>
>
> It is thus clear that, according to Sri SVBS, the DSV of Sri MS admits all
> the three sattAs, namely pAramArthika/vyAvahArika/prAtibhAsika.
>
>
>
> It appears therefore that the DSV of Sri MS does not adopt or endorse the
> two recognized DSVs namely DES (dRShTi eva sRShTi ) and DSKS (
> dRShTisamakAlInasRShTiH) which admit of only two sattAs namely
> pAramArthika and vyAvahArika. The version of Sri MS appears to be a third
> variety which, in my view, is very close to avasthAtrayavivEka.
>
