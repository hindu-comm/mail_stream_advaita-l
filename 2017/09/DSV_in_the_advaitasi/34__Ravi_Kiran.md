> At some places in shruti attributes creatorship to jeeva (example br.up.
> atha rathAn rathayOgAn pathaH srujate) and at some other place shruti says
> svapa katru is brahma..when jeeva is sleeping, this purusha is awake and he
> does the creation...(ya eshu supteshu jaagarti kAmam purushO nirmimANaH
> tadeva shukraM tadbrahma tadevaM amrutam uchyate) and again some other
> places shruti attributes svapna to the mind the upAdhi.  The samanvaya of
> this is, Ishwara is the one who gives svapna to jeeva through his upAdhi.
> If jeeva does only puNya kArya, if jeeva entertains only auspicious
> thoughts, if jeeva's thoughts and deeds are shubhra, accordingly Ishwara
> projects the svapna in jeeva's dream using manOpAdhi.  Hence svapna is the
> 'saMskAra vAsana' of jeeva which is the result of jeeva's saMskAra, karma
> in his waking state.  'As you sow shall you reap'.  The srushti by brahman
> / Ishwara also like this only shruti / shankara clarifies.  Ishwara does
> the srushti according to the various jeevas' karma.  The kAraNa for the
> vishama srushti for the Ishwara is various jeeva-s various  karma :' tadeva
> cha Ishwarasya phalahetutvaM yat svakarmAnurUpAH prajAH srujateeti.  For
> the svapna prapancha the domain is jeeva, upAdhi is jeevOpAdhi mana,
> material is jeeva saMskAra...And for the jAgrat prapancha (Ishwara srushti)
> the domain is hiraNyagarbha, brahman is the abhinna nimittOpadAna kAraNa
> for the jagat srushti and apekshe / purpose is : to allow jeeva-s to
> experience their karma phala.
