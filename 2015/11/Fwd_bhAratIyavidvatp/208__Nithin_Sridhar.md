> On the 24th Nov. 2015, there was a State level 'Spardhā' organized at the
> Purnaprajna Samshodhana Mandiram premises. A number of participants had
> come to take part in several competitions, to be selected for the finals
> that will be a future event.
>
> I had the fine opportunity and a delectable experience to hear four talks
> by PhD level scholars on the topic of 'Mokṣasvarūpam'.  The talks delivered
> were from the Nyaya, Purvamimamsa and Vedanta views.
>
> Sri Sirnidhi Pyāti, a scholar/teacher at the Vidyapīṭha spoke on the topic
> from the Advaita Vedanta angle.  He was declared the best speaker.
>
> Sri Shambhunatha Sharma, a research scholar in Vyakarana, spoke on the
> topic from the Advaita vedanta angle.  He was the second in the selection.
>
> Sri...Bhatta, from RSS, Sringeri, spoke from the Purvamimamsa angle.  He
> was the third in the selection.
>
> A female scholar from RSS Sringeri spoke from the Nyaya angle and this too
> was a fine, very informative, talk.  Unfortunately I had not recorded her
> speech.  It was only after her  talk, which was the first in order, that I
> thought of recording the talks.  Hence I have three recordings which I am
> presenting here for scholars to enjoy and get greatly informed by the
> really scholarly talks on the topic.
>
> I would like to add that Vidvan Srinidhi Pyati, though from the Madhva
> following, had bagged the first prize a few years ago in the RSS Shalaka
> pariksha on the Vivekachudamani, a well known work on Advaita.  He can be
> called an expert on that text.  In his present talk he has largely quoted
> from that text.
