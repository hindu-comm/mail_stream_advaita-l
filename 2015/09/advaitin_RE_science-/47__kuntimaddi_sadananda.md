I have not followed these discussions. Just read Bhaskar's out of curiosity. Here are some considerations;

1. One can perhaps know that the other person has understood shatras - if one considers that as shotriya. 

2. One cannot know if the other person is Brahma NishTa or not. Brahma nishta is not a discrete process but occurs slowly as the obstacles for the abiding knowledge slowly get dissolved. Hence as Swami Paramarthanandaji puts it is FIR reduction - The frequency of perturbation from the abiding knowledge, I - the intensity of perturbation and R- recovery time from perturbation - they slowly go down as one abides in the knowledge or one becomes Brahma Nishta. Hence only the individual knows if he is Brahma NishTa. 

3. The one who is Brahma NishTa will not declare he is one since other cannot judge - it is left to the faith of the others to recognize that my teacher is Brahma NishTa so that one has faith in the teaching 

4. Brahman Nishta does not really care if others recognize him as Brahman Nishta or not. Hence it is mute discussion from his point. It does not matter what he wares or goes about. 

5. The rest is only preparation for the purification of the mind and not get confused with the goal. 

na yogena na saakheyna karmaNaa no na vidyayaa
brahmaatmaika bodhena mokshaH sidhyati na anyathaa| - says Vivekachudamani. 

My 2c.

Hari Om!
Sadananda

 judgement of his character & behavior?? Anyway,
 kindly don’t take trouble to convince this mind, it will
 always be like that posing stupid
  questions due to lack of purity.
