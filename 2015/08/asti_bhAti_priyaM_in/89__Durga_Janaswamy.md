Hari Om,
Namaste.

Asti, jaDa (जड), is an object, being indriya grAhya, so asti is object of my consciousness.
Bhāti is not an object, it being not indriya grAhya, so bhāticannot be an object of my consciousness.

> (ड्र्ग्-द्र्स्य विवेक)

> asti bhAti priyaM rupaM nama chetyaMsa paMchakam 
> AdyatrayaM brahma rupaM jagad rupaM tato dvayam 
> (Drg-drsya viveka) 
>  
>  
> We know that chaitanyaM (चैतन्यं ) is not  indriya grAhya (इन्द्रिय ग्राह्य). 
>  
> Then how does bhAti (in the verse) is explained? 
>  
> It is the 'shine' that is there in every object which is revealed by  
> its becoming an object of knowledge for me. Asti gives the mere  
> 'existence'.  Bhāti is its becoming an object for my consciousness.  
>   'Ghaṭo'sti, ghaṭaḥ sphurati' brings out the difference between  
> existence and shine.  In Satyam Jnanam Anantam Brahma, the first is  
> asti and the second is bhāti.
