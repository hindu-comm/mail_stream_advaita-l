 Namaste,
Late Sastriji has desired that his incomplete work on his translation of part of the Panchapadika be taken up by the esteemed scholars and I am sure we have a number of eminent scholars who would be glad to do that. If permitted, may I voice a concern to say that we have to treat the Panchapadika more as Adi Shankara's own work than that of his disciple, and that is because it is said to have finally come out of Adi Shanka's own mukha-padma. If this is so the translator may have to keep out mind the views of Vacaspati Mishra and Mandana Mishra (the latter being the purvashrama personality of Sureshwaracharya himself). To my knowledge Adi Shankara and Padmapada were of the opinion that the Mahavakyas belonging to the Uttara-mimamsa create a sattvic frame of mind and can generate direct knowledge, whereas Vacaspati Mishra conrested that based on the views of Mandana Mishra's view's related to Purva-Mimansa. Purva-mimansa does need paroksha interpretation, which involves a sort of mental work before understanding / assimilation, but on the contrary the Vedanta has no such hang ups. I am however open to correction. 

Regards,Sunil KB
