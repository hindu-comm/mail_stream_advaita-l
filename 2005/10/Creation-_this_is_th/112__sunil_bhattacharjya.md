Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya,

The absolute is immutable in the permanent sense ie.
the absolute mutates in a reversible way only and that
too temporarily and reverts back to its original
immutable self at the end or dissolution of the
creation.

Sunil Bhattacharjya

> *"How is it, the Absolute which is immutable and
> which is beyond all guNas,
> resorts to action resulting in creation of the
> universe? Even as a Leela, is
> it not incompatible with the nirguNa nature of the
> Transcendental Absolute?"
> (III  7  2). *
> 
> * "You said, Oh Maitreya, that it is through avidyA
> (Cosmic Ignorance) that
> the Lord creates, sustains and dissolves the
> universe. But He the Almighty
> is beyond time and space. How did He associate
> Himself with avidyA? In all
> bodies the experiencer that is the jIva is also
> nothing but a spark of the
> Absolute. How can he then lose the Bliss that he
> came from and how can he
> suffer because of actions?" (III  7  4 to 6).*
> 
> * Maitreya replies (III  7  9):*
> 
> * seyaM bhagavato mAyA yanna yena virudhyate / *

> 
> * In fact, it appears to be a contradiction that to
> the Lord who is devoid
> of all avidyA, there happens due to avidyA a bondage
> and a fall of
> Knowledge. "*
