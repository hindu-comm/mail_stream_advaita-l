शिव एव गुरुः साक्षात् गुरुरेव शिवः स्वयम् ।
उभयोरन्तरं किञ्चिन्न द्रष्टव्यं मुमुक्षुभिः ॥ २५६ ॥

Shiva alone is the Guru and the Guru himself is Shiva.  The aspirant aught
not to perceive difference between the two.

धन्यः कृतार्थस्त्वमहो विवेकः
शिवप्रसादस्तव विद्यते महान् ।
विसृज्य तु प्राकृतलोकमार्गं
ब्रह्मावगन्तुं यतसे यतस्त्वम् ॥ २७८ ॥

The Guru praises the disciple for having taken to the path of Atma vidya.
He says: You have been graced by Shiva and hence alone you have chosen this
path having renounced he worldly accomplishments.

  शिवप्रसादेन विना न सिद्धिः
शिवप्रसादेन विना न बुद्धिः ।
शिवप्रसादेन विना न युक्तिः
शिवप्रसादेन विना न मुक्तिः ॥ २७९ ॥

Without Shiva's grace the following are impossible to get:  Siddhi: the
fruition of our endeavor. Buddhi: the right intellect to tread this path.
Yukti: the proper reasoning for advancing in this path. Mukti: liberation.

  यस्य प्रसादेन विमुक्तसङ्गाः
शुकादयः संसृतिबन्धमुक्ताः ।
तस्य प्रसादो बहुजन्मलभ्यो
भक्त्यैकगम्यो भवमुक्तिहेतुः ॥ २८० ॥

It is by striving over several births does one get the grace of Shiva by
whose intervention Shuka, etc. became liberated. By devotion to Shiva alone
one can get liberated.

The Sukha episode is in this shuka rahasya upanishad.
